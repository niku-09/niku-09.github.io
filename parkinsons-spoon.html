Self-Stabilizing Spoon
for Parkinson's Patients

Overview

Parkinson's disease patients have significantly impaired fine motor control, making self-feeding difficult without assistance. Since they tremor a lot, even eating a bowl of porridge with a spoon without spillage becomes a task!

This project is a handheld active stabilization system designed to reduce tremor-induced motion during eating.

Team Members: Nikhil Mohan, Abhinav Shaji Kumar, Nekha Sudheer

Design & CAD

The device was designed from the ground up ” starting with a custom CAD-modelled casing that houses the gimbal mechanism and electronics in a compact, handheld form factor.

Electronics & Software

The electronics consist of an MPU6050 IMU, two servo motors driving a two-axis gimbal, a LiPo battery, and a buck converter for stable power distribution across components. The IMU continuously samples hand orientation and the servos compensate for unintended movement in real time.

The system runs on Arduino and uses quaternion-based orientation tracking to avoid gimbal lock and ensure smooth, accurate angle representation. Filtering and control loop optimizations were applied to minimize servo jitter and improve overall stabilization smoothness.

Future / Current Directions

The system is evolving from a reactive stabilization approach towards a hybrid predictive-reactive architecture, using an on-device TinyML model to classify and anticipate tremor patterns for smoother, more adaptive compensation.
