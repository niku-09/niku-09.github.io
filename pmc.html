Porous Media Combustion

This project has been close to three years in the making. I designed, fabricated, and commissioned the entire experimental setup myself ” welding the burner housing, threading pipe fittings, integrating valves, wiring the DAQ, and calibrating flow meters. Every part of the rig was built in-house, from scratch (fully funded by the university ” â‚¹3“4 lakhs).

The research explores Porous Media Burners (PMBs) ” a combustion technology where the flame is stabilised within a ceramic foam matrix rather than burning freely in open air. The porous structure enables significantly leaner combustion, more uniform temperature distribution, lower emissions, and suppressed flame instabilities that plague conventional burners.

I tested multiple ceramic foam materials and configurations, instrumenting each run with thermocouples, a gas analyser, thermal imaging, and acoustic sensors to characterise how the porous medium changes the fundamental behaviour and characteristics of the flame.
